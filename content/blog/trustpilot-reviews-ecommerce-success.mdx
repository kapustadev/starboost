While Google Maps dominates local search and Facebook dominates social, **Trustpilot** is the undisputed king of e-commerce and global service reviews.

## The Trustpilot Widget Effect

Trustpilot is unique because businesses actively embed its widget on their own websites. Placing a dynamic "Excellent 4.8/5" Trustpilot badge on your checkout page is proven to reduce cart abandonment rates by up to 30%.

But what happens if your rating drops to "Poor" or "Average"? The widget becomes a liability.

## The Challenge with Trustpilot

Trustpilot has one of the strictest filtering algorithms in the industry. It actively flags and removes reviews it deems suspicious. This makes it incredibly difficult for businesses to naturally recover from a wave of unfair negative reviews or competitor sabotage.

Even worse, angry customers are highly motivated to create Trustpilot accounts just to leave a 1-star review, while happy customers rarely bother.
